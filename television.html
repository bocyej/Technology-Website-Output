 TELEVISION (TV)

Television is a telecommunication medium for transmitting and receiving moving images that can be monochrome or colored, with or without accompanying sound.  The most basic idea of a television is "radio with pictures". In other words, where radio transmits sound signal through the air, and the television sends a picture signal.

When the first TV was developed, it can only show black and white pictures.  Nowadays, modern TVs can transmit multicolored images through the combination of the three primary colors, red, green, blue (RGB).  Smart TVs can also connect to the internet, which allows users to stream music and videos, browse the internet, and view photos.  It contains an operating system that provides access to applications and other digital content.



  Why are Televisions amazing?

Televisions provide a great source of entertainment.  It can also be a source of good quality TV content, and provide young audience witha guided viewing experience and teach them to have critical thinking skills they need to be active, engaged viewers.  Aside from entertainment, TVs can provide us with new information and connect us to the world, through news and other documentaries shows.  Watching TV can also provide a break from our everyday life, keeping our minds off and recharge, having an immersive experience that takes us out of our everyday life and troubles.  After watching, we often feel refreshed and more ready to tackle different life demands.
